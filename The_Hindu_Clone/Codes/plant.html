How does a plant’s first shoot rise safely through soil, into daylight?

Researchers from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER), Bhopal, have found that a single protein helps plants time their first step from darkness into light. When a seed sprouts in darkness under the soil, its stem curves into a small hook shape that protects the delicate shoot tip as it pushes upward. The hook needs to stay ‘closed’ until the seedling breaks through the soil and meets light. In the study, the team wanted to know how two common signals — ethylene, a plant hormone that builds up underground, and light — work together to decide exactly when the hook opens.
